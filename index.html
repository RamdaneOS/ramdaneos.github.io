Bio

I am an Environmental Economist with a passion for utilizing econometrics and data science methods to address issues at the nexus of society and the environment. I am currently conducting my doctoral research jointly at United Nations University-FLORES and the Technical University of Dresden in Germany. My thesis aims to explain causal mechanisms leading to social sustainability and measure progress toward this goal. My research interests span from development economics, environmental resources management, to sustainability assessment, with a focus on the agrifood sector.

I have over four years of experience in data analytics and modelling, having worked as a Researcher for the African Union Commission, a Data Fellow at UNFPA and a Research Analyst at IFAD. I contributed to UNFPA's work on Civil Registration and Vital Statistics, using complex survey, census, and registration data to predict and adjust the determinants of vital events' registration with a gender-sensitive lens. I also supported IFAD's Independent Office of Evaluation in undertaking a sub-regional evaluation of IFAD operations in countries with fragile situations in West Africa, through data collection, analysis, visualization, and preliminary reporting.

I am currently pursuing a PhD in Integrated Management of Environmental Resources from Technische Universität Dresden, where I also obtained a Postgraduate Diploma in Environmental Management. In addition, I completed a Sustainable Finance program at Frankfurt School of Finance & Management. I have multiple certifications in Data Science, Public Management, Sustainable Land Management, and Social and Economic Networks Analysis, as well as a Best Final Paper Award for my Postgraduate work on Participatory Forest Management in Benin. I am fluent in French and English, and I am learning German. I am passionate about using data science to understand and model socio-ecological dynamics and to inform evidence-based policies and interventions that foster social sustainability and environmental protection. I am eager to collaborate with and learn from other researchers and practitioners in this field and to contribute to the global agenda of sustainable development.

Sensitive Social Issues
on CRVS in Burkina Faso

I worked with UNFPA on a research project on Civil Registration and Vital Statistics (CRVS). We looked at the proximate determinants of the under-registration of vital events and provided evidence to government institutions working in vital statistics production.

As part of this project, I did an exploratory analysis to understand some of the key issues discussed by local online media on Birth, Marriage and Death registration in Burkina Faso.

Read more here: Sensitive Social Issues on CRVS in Burkina Faso .

You can also have a look at this poster on the topic: Understanding Barriers to Vital Events Registration in Burkina Faso through Language Use .

iCivil: A smartphone-based
birth registration
technology in Burkina Faso

I supported the digitalization of the CRVS system in Burkina Faso through the deployment of "iCivil", a platform for systematic registration of vital events (Birth, Marriage, Death) information.

I did a preliminary analysis of the first ever data export from the iCivil server here: Analysis of iCivil Server Birth Registration Data .
